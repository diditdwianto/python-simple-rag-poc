## 1. Zorbx

**Human equivalent:** Pizza

Zorbx is the most universally beloved snack in the outer rim — a flat, round base
of skorbian crust topped with savory glorp sauce, melted vellum-cheese, and whatever
toppings the local system provides. Every station and moon has its own variation.

### Ingredients

- **Skorbian crust mix** — a flour made from ground skorbian root, high in starch
  and naturally elastic when hydrated. 300g per Zorbx.
- **Glorp sauce** — a tangy, deep-red paste made from fermented glorpberries found
  only on Glorpia Major. 200ml per Zorbx.
- **Vellum-cheese** — a soft, stretchy cheese analogue derived from the milk of
  vellum-beasts (six-legged herbivores native to Vellumia). Shreds beautifully and
  melts into long strings. 150g per Zorbx.
- **Toppings (choose one or more):**
  - **Spicy rumblenuts** — small, crunchy, peppery nuts from the Rumblenut tree.
  - **Seared qworm strips** — protein-rich worm-like delicacies, mild flavor,
    satisfying chew.
  - **Frozen nubla pearls** — sweet, burst-in-mouth spheres that add a fruity
    contrast.

### Recipe

1. Preheat your thermal chamber to 220°C (or 7 on a Mark-IV plasma oven).
2. Mix skorbian crust mix with 150ml warm plasma-water. Knead for 5 minutes until
   the dough springs back when poked. Cover and let rest for 20 minutes.
3. Roll the dough into a 30cm round disc on a floured surface. The crust should be
   about 5mm thick at the center, slightly thicker at the edges to hold the toppings.
4. Spread glorp sauce evenly over the base, leaving a 1cm border for the crust edge.
5. Scatter vellum-cheese shreds over the sauce. Distribute your chosen toppings.
6. Bake in the thermal chamber for 12–15 minutes until the vellum-cheese is bubbling
   and the crust edges are golden-brown.
7. Let cool for 2 minutes, slice into 6 triangular sectors, and serve immediately.

### Recommended occasion

Zorbx is the go-to snack for **cargo bay movie nights** and **post-mission
debriefings**. Its shareable format makes it perfect for crews of 3–8 who want
something warm and satisfying without a formal meal. Pairs exceptionally well with
a cold tankard of fizzstout or a glass of sparkling nebula-ade.

## 2. Kryzzle

**Human equivalent:** Burger

The Kryzzle is a hearty, hand-held snack consisting of a thick protein patty
sandwiched between two halves of a glazed bloom-bun. Juicy, messy, and deeply
filling — it is the staple of spaceport food courts across the Orion Cascade.

### Ingredients

- **Bloom-bun** — a sweet, slightly sticky bread made from bloom-grain flour.
  The buns are flash-grilled on the inside to create a caramelized seal that
  prevents sogginess. 1 bun (2 halves) per Kryzzle.
- **Grond patty** — a ground meat from the grond beast, a large four-horned
  grazer found on Grondia-IV. Rich in umami and natural juices. 200g per patty.
  For vegetarian crews, a blended qworm-and-spore patty works as a substitute.
- **Zing-sauce** — a sharp, citrus-like condiment squeezed from the rind of the
  zing-fruit. Adds a tangy kick that cuts through the richness of the grond patty.
- **Crisp fronds** — fresh, crunchy green leaves from the frond-fern, similar in
  texture to terrestrial lettuce but with a slight peppery kick.
- **Pickled gherk-spores** — fermented gherk-spore pods that offer a sour, crunchy
  contrast.

### Recipe

1. Season the grond patty with a pinch of asteroid salt and cracked vim-pepper.
   Form into a round disc slightly wider than the bloom-bun (it shrinks during
   cooking).
2. Grill the patty on a plasma griddle at 200°C for 4 minutes per side. Do not
   press down — the juices must stay inside. The patty is done when the juices
   run clear with a faint purple tint.
3. While the patty grills, halve the bloom-bun and place the halves face-down on
   the griddle for 1 minute until the cut surface is caramelized and golden.
4. Assemble: bottom bun → zing-sauce → crisp fronds → grond patty → pickled
   gherk-spores → zing-sauce (yes, twice — once on each side of the patty) → top bun.
5. Serve immediately while the patty is still steaming. One Kryzzle per crew member.

### Recommended occasion

Kryzzles are best served during **mid-shift break on long hauls** when crew morale
needs a boost. Also a fixture at **spaceport diners** and **victory celebrations**
after successful cargo runs. Pairs with a tall glass of iced starhopper ale or a
simple cup of hot groxl.

## 3. Splink

**Human equivalent:** Hotdog

Splinks are the quick, cheap, eaten-one-handed snack of the galaxy. A skewered
blorben sausage nestled in a soft, slightly sweet ripple-roll, dripping with
mustard-mist and crunchy dust-crumbles. Found at every station kiosk, shuttle
stop, and concert venue.

### Ingredients

- **Blorben sausage** — a long, mildly spiced sausage made from blended blorben
  meat (a small, fast-breeding rodent-analogue farmed on Blorben Station). Smoked
  over grom-wood chips. 1 per Splink.
- **Ripple-roll** — a soft, slightly sweet bread roll with a characteristic rippled
  surface. Made from ripple-grain flour. The roll is slit down the top to tuck the
  sausage in. 1 per Splink.
- **Mustard-mist** — a spray-on condiment made from powdered yello-mustard seeds
  mixed with spark-water. Delivers a tangy, slightly fizzy mustard sensation.
- **Dust-crumbles** — crispy, salty crumbles made from fried dust-potato shavings.
  Sprinkled on top for texture.

### Recipe

1. Bring a pot of brine-salt water to a rolling boil. Drop in the blorben sausages
   and simmer for 6 minutes. Remove and pat dry.
2. Alternatively, grill the sausages over open flame for a smokier flavor — 3
   minutes per side until the casing blisters slightly.
3. Warm the ripple-roll briefly in a steam chamber (30 seconds) so it becomes soft
   and pillowy without losing its shape.
4. Slice the ripple-roll lengthwise, leaving a hinge at the bottom so it holds the
   sausage.
5. Place the blorben sausage inside the roll. Spray mustard-mist generously over
   the top. Scatter dust-crumbles over the condiment.
6. Serve on a wax-paper liner. Eat standing up, one-handed, while watching a pod-race.

### Recommended occasion

Splinks are the quintessential **shuttle-stop snack** — quick, cheap, and eaten
on the move. They are also the official food of **pod-racing events** and
**station deck parties** where people mingle standing up. Pairs with fizzy
star-cola or a small cup of broth-steep.

## 4. Phuffin

**Human equivalent:** Popcorn

Phuffin is the sound these puffy little morsels make when they burst open in the
heat. Made from phuffin kernels — tiny, hollow seeds from the phuffin plant that
explode into fluffy, crunchy bites when heated. The universal snack for
entertainment consumption.

### Ingredients

- **Phuffin kernels** — small, round seeds harvested from the phuffin plant on
  Phuffin's World III. Each kernel has a hollow core that expands 40× when heated.
  100g yields approximately 4 liters of popped phuffin. Available in natural (pale
  gold) and crimson varieties.
- **Flavor coatings (pick one or mix):**
  - **Meteor-salt blend** — coarse salt crystals mixed with mineral flakes that give
    a savory, slightly smoky taste. The classic choice.
  - **Sweet nebula-dust** — fine, sparkly powder made from crystallized nebula-sap.
    Tastes like caramel with a hint of cinnamon.
  - **Spicy vortex-chili** — a fiery coating made from ground vortex-peppers. Not
    for the faint-hearted.
  - **Garlic-butter asteroid blend** — rich, buttery coating with roasted garlic
    flakes and asteroid salt. The fan favorite.

### Recipe

1. Heat a deep cooking vessel with 2 tablespoons of bloom-oil over medium-high heat.
   The oil should shimmer but not smoke.
2. Add 3–4 phuffin kernels as testers and cover with a lid. When they pop, the oil
   is at the right temperature.
3. Pour in the remaining kernels (about 100g for a standard crew sharing bowl).
   Cover immediately with the lid.
4. Shake the vessel every 20 seconds to prevent burning. When the popping slows to
   1 pop per 3 seconds, remove from heat.
5. Let rest for 30 seconds (stragglers may still pop). Remove the lid carefully.
6. Drizzle with melted bloom-butter and toss. Sprinkle your chosen flavor coating
   generously and toss again until evenly coated.
7. Transfer to a large communal bowl. Best consumed immediately while warm and
   maximally crunchy.

### Recommended occasion

Phuffin is inseparable from **holo-cinema screenings**, **command deck
marathons**, and **late-night cargo bay gambling sessions**. Its light, addictive
nature means a bowl disappears in minutes. Pairs with any cold beverage — fizzy
star-cola, iced nebula-ade, or a simple splash-juice. Mandatory snack for
**fleet-wide movie nights**.

## 5. Flapjack Nebula-Stack

**Human equivalent:** Pancake

The Flapjack Nebula-Stack is a towering breakfast spectacle — layers of soft,
spongy moon-flour pancakes stacked high and drenched in luminous glow-syrup.
A beloved morning ritual on every space station from here to the Outer Veil.

### Ingredients

- **Moon-flour** — a fine, silvery flour milled from moon-grain, a crop grown in
  low-gravity hydroponic bays. Produces exceptionally light, airy batter. 250g.
- **Plasma-milk** — a rich, slightly luminescent milk from plasma-cows bred on the
  orbital ranches of Plasma-7. Gives the pancakes a subtle蓝色 tint and creamy
  flavor. 300ml.
- **Glow-syrup** — the signature topping. A sweet, amber syrup tapped from glow-trees
  on Luminara. Glows faintly under ultraviolet light — a breakfast showstopper.
  Pour generously.
- **Bloom-butter** — rich, golden butter made from bloom-cream. For greasing the
  pan and melting over the top. 30g.
- **Fresh nubla berries** — small, sweet, purple berries added between layers for
  bursts of fruitiness. 100g.
- **Optional: powdered star-dust** — a shimmering sugar substitute made from
  crystallized star-sap, sprinkled over the top for visual flair and extra sweetness.

### Recipe

1. In a mixing bowl, whisk moon-flour, a pinch of asteroid salt, 1 tablespoon of
   bloom-oil, and the plasma-milk until smooth. The batter should be pourable but
   not watery — add more moon-flour or plasma-milk to adjust. Let it rest for 10
   minutes.
2. Heat a flat griddle or plasma pan over medium heat. Rub with a small cube of
   bloom-butter.
3. Pour approximately 60ml of batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the
   surface and the edges look set (about 2 minutes). Flip and cook for another
   1–1.5 minutes until golden on both sides.
4. Repeat until all batter is used. You should get 6–8 pancakes per batch.
5. Assemble the stack: place one pancake on a plate, scatter a few nubla berries
   and a drizzle of glow-syrup, then place the next pancake on top. Repeat until
   all pancakes are stacked.
6. Top with a generous pour of glow-syrup, a pat of bloom-butter, and a dusting
   of powdered star-dust if desired.
7. Serve immediately while the stack is warm and the syrup is cascading down the
   sides. Provide extra syrup on the side for those who want more.

### Recommended occasion

Flapjack Nebula-Stack is the hero of **lazy morning cycles on the space station**,
**crew birthday breakfasts**, and **post-hypersleep recovery meals**. The glow of
the syrup under cabin lights makes it particularly popular during **dim-lit
morning mess** where the visual spectacle is half the experience. Pairs with a
mug of hot steem-coffee or a glass of freshly pressed nubla juice.

## 6. Crispx

**Human equivalent:** Potato chips / crisps

Crispx are thin, impossibly crunchy slices of dust-potato fried in bloom-oil and
dusted with bold flavor coatings. Sold in crinkly foil pouches at every depot,
cantina, and vending machine across the galaxy. Impossible to eat just one pouch.

### Ingredients

- **Dust-potatoes** — small, dense tubers grown in the mineral-rich dust-soil of
  Dustara. Their high starch content makes them ideal for frying into a satisfying
  crunch. 500g (about 4–5 medium tubers).
- **Bloom-oil** — a light, neutral oil pressed from bloom-seeds. High smoke point
  makes it perfect for deep frying. 1 liter for frying.
- **Asteroid salt** — coarse, mineral-rich salt mined from asteroid fields. Gives a
  complex, slightly earthy salinity. Essential base seasoning.
- **Flavor dust (choose one or create your own):**
  - **Nebula-ranch** — tangy, herbaceous powder with hints of dried vellum-cream
    and star-herbs. The galaxy's best-selling flavor.
  - **Volcano-spice** — a five-spice blend from the volcanic kitchens of Ignara.
    Sweet, smoky, and deeply savory.
  - **Quantum-cheese** — bright orange powder that tastes like concentrated
    vellum-cheese with a sharp, tangy finish. Loud and addictive.
  - **Sweet cosmo-caramel** — for the sweetcravers. Caramelized nebula-sap dusted
    over salted crispx.

### Recipe

1. Peel the dust-potatoes and slice them as thinly as possible (1–2mm). A mandoline
   slicer or laser-knife attachment yields the most consistent results.
2. Soak the slices in cold plasma-water for at least 30 minutes to remove excess
  starch. This step is critical for achieving maximum crunch.
3. Drain and pat completely dry with lint-free cloth. Any moisture left on the
  slices causes violent spattering in the oil.
4. Heat bloom-oil to 175°C in a deep fry vessel. Fry the slices in small batches
  (do not overcrowd) for 3–4 minutes until golden and the bubbling subsides.
5. Remove with a slotted scoop and drain on absorbent mesh. While still hot and
  slightly glistening with oil, immediately sprinkle with asteroid salt and your
  chosen flavor dust. Toss to coat evenly.
6. Allow to cool completely — crispx become fully crunchy as they cool. Store in
  sealed foil pouches to maintain crispness. Consume within 48 hours for peak
  quality (though most pouches do not survive that long).

### Recommended occasion

Crispx are the universal **anytime snack**. They appear at **card games in the
recreation bay**, **late-night watch shifts**, **road trips through asteroid
belts**, and **casual hangouts on the observation deck**. They travel well and
require zero preparation, making them a staple in every crew's provisions locker.
Pairs with cold fizzstout, star-cola, or simply a glass of filtered ice-water
after a long shift.

## 7. Moonscroll

**Human equivalent:** Croissant / pastry roll

Moonscrolls are elegant, flaky pastry spirals made from laminated moon-flour dough
and bloom-butter. Each layer separates into impossibly thin sheets when pulled apart,
releasing a subtle aroma of toasted bloom-grain. A staple of station cafés and
fancy brunch spots.

### Ingredients

- **Moon-flour** — 400g, the same light, silvery flour used in Flapjack Nebula-Stacks.
  Its low-gluten nature creates the signature tender layers.
- **Bloom-butter** — 250g, kept cold. This is the fat that creates the flaky layers.
  Must be cold but pliable for proper lamination.
- **Plasma-milk** — 120ml, for hydrating the dough.
- **Star-yeast** — 7g, a fast-acting yeast strain cultivated on Starwell Station.
  Gives a mild, pleasant rise in about 45 minutes.
- **Asteroid salt** — 1 teaspoon.
- **Fillings (optional, choose one):**
  - **Glow-cream filling** — sweet, custard-like cream infused with glow-syrup.
  - **Spiced grom-fruit paste** — a tart, aromatic fruit preserve from the grom-fruit
    orchards of Gromia.
  - **Savory vellum-cheese and qworm** — for those who prefer their moonscrolls
    non-sweet.

### Recipe

1. Dissolve star-yeast and 1 tablespoon of star-sugar (or nebula-sap) in warm
   plasma-milk. Let sit for 5 minutes until frothy.
2. Mix moon-flour, asteroid salt, and the yeast mixture into a smooth dough. Knead
   briefly (3 minutes) until elastic but not overworked. Shape into a disc, wrap,
   and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
3. Prepare the butter block: place cold bloom-butter between two sheets of
   parchment. Pound and roll into a 15cm × 15cm square. Keep refrigerated until
   needed.
4. Roll the chilled dough into a rectangle roughly twice the size of the butter
   block. Place the butter block in the center and fold the dough over it like an
   envelope, sealing the edges.
5. Roll out the dough-butter envelope into a long rectangle (about 60cm × 20cm).
   Fold into thirds (like a letter). This is one "turn." Wrap and refrigerate for
   20 minutes. Repeat for a total of 3 turns with 20-minute rests between each.
6. After the final rest, roll the dough into a large rectangle about 5mm thick. If
   using filling, spread it evenly across the surface. Cut into long triangles
   (base ~10cm) and roll each triangle from base to tip into a crescent shape.
7. Place on a lined baking tray, curve the tips inward to form the classic crescent
   shape. Proof in a warm, humid spot for 45 minutes until puffy and visibly risen.
8. Brush with plasma-milk for a golden finish. Bake in a thermal chamber at 190°C
   for 15–18 minutes until deeply golden and flaky.
9. Cool for 10 minutes. Best eaten warm, pulled apart layer by layer.

### Recommended occasion

Moonscrolls are a **station café luxury** — the kind of breakfast that makes a
morning feel special. They are served at **officer brunches**, **diplomatic
breakfasts on Nebulon Prime**, and **weekend morning treat cycles** where the
crew actually has time to enjoy pastry rather than gulp ration bars. Pairs
beautifully with a proper cup of steem-coffee or a glass of chilled plasma-milk.
